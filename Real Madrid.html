The history of Real Madrid football club



Real Madrid Club de Fútbol is a renowned football club based in Madrid, Spain. Founded on March 6, 1902, the club was granted the title “Real” (meaning “Royal”) by King Alfonso XIII in 1920. Its home stadium, the Santiago Bernabéu, is one of the most iconic football venues in the world. Real Madrid is widely regarded as one of the most successful football clubs in history, having won over 30 La Liga titles and a record 15 UEFA Champions League trophies. The club is famous for attracting world-class players such as Alfredo Di Stéfano, Zinedine Zidane, Ronaldo, Cristiano Ronaldo, and Luka Modrić. Beyond their on-pitch achievements, Real Madrid has played a crucial role in shaping global football, influencing both the business and cultural aspects of the sport. Their historic rivalry with FC Barcelona, known as “El Clásico,” remains one of the most highly anticipated and watched matches in world football.
